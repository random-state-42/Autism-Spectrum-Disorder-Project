Core Behavioral Features

Social Communication and Interaction

Eye Contact

Limited or atypical eye contact during interactions

Apparent in infants as young as 6 months

Social Smiling

Delayed or infrequent smiling in response to others

Joint Attention

Difficulty sharing focus on objects or events with others

Key marker at 9-15 months

Response to Name

Consistently not responding when name is called

Significant by 12 months

Restricted and Repetitive Behaviors

Stereotyped Movements

Repetitive body movements (hand flapping, rocking, spinning)

Insistence on Sameness

Extreme distress at small changes in routine or environment

Highly Restricted Interests

Intense focus on specific topics or objects

Sensory Sensitivities

Unusual reactions to sounds, textures, lights, or smells

Advanced Detection Features

Language and Communication

Speech Patterns

Delayed speech development or atypical speech patterns

Echolalia

Repeating words or phrases without communicative intent

Pragmatic Language

Difficulty with conversational turn-taking and topic maintenance

Play Skills

Imaginative Play

Limited pretend or imaginative play

Emerges typically by 18-24 months

Functional Play

Repetitive or rigid use of toys (lining up, spinning parts)

Social Play

Limited interest in playing with peers

Technological Detection Methods

Questionnaire-Based Assessment

Evaluates responses from a set of 10 targeted questions to identify potential signs of autism and assess risk levels.
